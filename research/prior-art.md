# Prior Art

Existing games and tools that overlap with the MMODAW design, and what to learn from each.

---

## IV-V-I: The Harmony Card Game

A physical card game where players compete to build the best harmonic progressions using good voice leading and cadences. The closest analog to what Scale Navigator encodes: functional harmony as an accessible, symbol-based system playable by non-theorists.

- **Format:** 2–4 players, 30–60 min, ages 12+, $10 (currently sold out)
- **Creator:** Dr. Rafael Hernandez — composer, Professor of Music and Deputy Provost at CSU East Bay; his work centers on music, technology, and gaming. Also creator of the MusicEd font family.
- **Publisher:** MusicTeacherTools, a small California company (operating since 2007) whose main line is the MusicEd font family (typeable rhythms, Kodály stick notation, hand signs, and fingering diagrams).
- **Source:** https://musicteachertools.myshopify.com/products/iv-v-i

### Mechanics

- Each player is dealt 7 cards. On a turn, draw from the draw or discard pile, then build a phrase of up to three harmony cards, played to the left or right of your existing phrase.
- Harmony cards are labeled by function — **T** (tonic), **S** (subdominant / pre-dominant), **D** (dominant) — and each card has a **"play window"** dictating what can precede and follow it (a "5" card can be preceded by a T or S and followed by a T). This encodes real voice-leading grammar as a constraint system.
- **Special Harmony cards** (rainbow-colored) like "sequence by fifths" and "chordal expansion" add flexibility.
- **Style cards** (named after composers) change scoring rules — e.g. the Shostakovich card inverts the usual penalty logic, rewarding part-writing errors instead of penalizing them.

### Reception

Warm but niche. Praise on r/musictheory ("incredible," "great for beginners… common-practice harmonies"), house rules for tone rows and jazz ii-V-I, and wishes for Wagner / Schoenberg / Stravinsky cards. No BoardGameGeek listing or formal press reviews found.

### What to take from it

- The **"play window"** is exactly our harmonic-transition constraint — the same idea Ensemble Jammer uses to keep every instrument inside the shared harmonic state. Proof that a constraint system on transitions reads as a *game* to non-theorists.
- **Symbol-based, training-free access** validates the "no music theory required" premise.
- **Style cards** are a scoring / ruleset mod — a lightweight way to give the same harmonic material different flavor and replayability.
- It confirms there is appetite for harmony-as-game, and that existing offerings are physical, turn-based, and academic — leaving the real-time, playable, artifact-producing space wide open.
