## TL;DR

*Ultimate Drummer* is the debut video game from **Cool 3D World** — the Brooklyn animation/music duo **Jon Baken and Brian Tessler**, famous since the Vine era for surreal, grotesque 3D shorts (and the "ham documentary" in *Smiling Friends*). It's a short (1–2 hour), self-funded, Unreal Engine **action-platformer "DRPG"** in which you play an exiled drummer restoring harmony to a fractured world of music gods. Its signature hook is a **fully playable step-sequencer drum kit** that runs continuously as you glide, platform, and fight through soulslike/*Shadow of the Colossus*/*Zelda*/*Journey*-inspired environments. Critics and streamers loved it (Steam "Very Positive," ~87–99% positive across snapshots; Kotaku, Iron Pineapple, Noclip praise), but reach stayed tiny (all-time peak ~38 concurrent players) — a textbook "critically adored, commercially niche" indie. The creators themselves say it's "**much more of an exploration 3D platformer than a rhythm game.**"

Notably relevant to your own work: this is one of the very few games where **music-making is the mechanic** rather than note-matching — placing it in a rare lineage (Fract OSC, Sound Shapes, Electroplankton) that overlaps conceptually with the Scale Navigator "explore → capture → share" thesis, but wrapped in combat and platforming none of those did.

## 1. What it is — gameplay & mechanics

Official description: an action-platformer DRPG where "you play as a lone drummer on a sacred quest to restore harmony to a fractured world," using "a fully playable step-sequencer drum kit to reshape reality, awaken lost musicians, and bring music back to life" ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3646020/Ultimate_Drummer/)).

**Core loop & features** ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3646020/Ultimate_Drummer/)):
- **Narrative-driven progression** — "unfold the mythos of the music Gods and restore harmony through ritual, rhythm, platforming, and combat." ~1–2 hours of gameplay.
- **Fully playable step-sequencer drum kit** — "create rhythms freely and shape the world through tempo and timing." The beat runs as an uninterrupted loop while you play; you customize it, save presets, and unlock new samples ([Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/cool-3d-world-unleash-their-beefy-musical-soulslike-ultimate-drummer-2000690458)).
- **Open-ended musical sandbox** — "sequence up to 6 musicians simultaneously to create your own masterful compositions, with animations synced to your every note."
- **Deep character customization** — a character creator "capable of rendering many miracles, except for delivering a normal skull shape" ([Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/cool-3d-world-unleash-their-beefy-musical-soulslike-ultimate-drummer-2000690458)); unlockable clothing and drum-kit skins.
- **23 Steam achievements**, single-player, Steam Cloud, macOS + Windows (Steam Deck: SteamOS-compatible, not Deck-Verified) ([SteamDB](https://steamdb.info/app/3646020/)).

**Story** ([Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/cool-3d-world-unleash-their-beefy-musical-soulslike-ultimate-drummer-2000690458)): a supergroup of gods once found "a most excellent harmony," then exiled their drummer for growing too powerful. He honed his skills and returned to banish the other gods. As a new hero with "the divine powers of the sticks," you rescue each member of heaven's band to lift the blight.

**Feel & traversal** ([Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/cool-3d-world-unleash-their-beefy-musical-soulslike-ultimate-drummer-2000690458)): gliding on your drum set across "deathtrap dungeons," Sonic-like speed-boost rings, and "mountains of white plastic lawn chairs"; "Janky cave perverts," gun-toting ogres, and "surprisingly intense boss battles" (Kotaku likens some to *Elden Ring*'s Silver Sphere drops).

**How the creators describe the genre** ([Noclip interview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8h3GW4Ze7pM)):
> Tessler: "I usually start with '3D platformer,' and then I'll say, 'But, you're a drummer.'"
> Baken: "It's a game where you play drums, too… you can sequence a drum set and move around in a 3D space… you're playing a drummer who's drumming."
> Tessler: "If we're being honest, it's much more of an exploration 3D platformer game than… you'd have to kind of make an argument to call it a rhythm game." The drum kit "does control things around you to a degree."

**Cited inspirations:** FromSoftware/Souls games, *Shadow of the Colossus*, *Zelda*, *Journey* ([Noclip](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8h3GW4Ze7pM)). Noclip frames it in the "less-you-know-the-better" tradition of *Frog Fractions* and *The Stanley Parable*.

## 2. The people — Jon Baken & Brian Tessler (Cool 3D World)

**Cool 3D World** is a Brooklyn multimedia studio founded ~2015 by Baken and Tessler, both **musicians with no prior visual-arts background**. Introduced by a mutual friend over a shared love of electronic music, they made a music video together — the start of the project — and broke out with 6-second surreal 3D shorts on Vine (first Vine Sept 28, 2015), later moving to YouTube/Instagram/Facebook. Signature style: grotesque, uncanny, morphed 3D figures in mundane settings ([Noclip](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8h3GW4Ze7pM); [Know Your Meme](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/sites/cool-3d-world); [cool3dworld.com/about](https://cool3dworld.com/about)).

**Following (2026):** ~1M YouTube subscribers, ~500K Instagram, ~1.6M Facebook ([Noclip](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8h3GW4Ze7pM); [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/cool3dworld/)). Their short *Chefs* has 40M+ views ([It's Nice That](https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/cool-3d-world-animation-250719)).

## 3. How the game was made

Per the **Noclip documentary** ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8h3GW4Ze7pM)):
- During COVID lockdown, Baken had free time; his roommate **Gabe** showed him **Unreal Engine**, and he started experimenting. He pushed Tessler to treat game dev as "the next thing," even though it meant abandoning ~8 years of Cinema 4D mastery to "be trash at software again."
- They considered hiring a programmer but chose to **learn Unreal themselves** via the visual **Blueprint** system. Baken found Blueprints intuitive because "the visual language… felt really similar to modular synths, like patching" — a direct bridge from his music-production skillset. (They rejected Unity because it "felt like too much to learn" without code.)
- One of Baken's earliest Unreal sketches used **state machines and layered bone animation** to give the character independent-moving arms — perfect for drumming — with a jump on spacebar. This "drummer who's drumming" seed stood out when they tested a mix of prototypes on friends.
- Development stretched across multiple years, picked up between paying gigs.

## 4. Funding, investors & business model

**Bottom line: there was no external funding, no investors, and no publisher.** *Ultimate Drummer* is a **self-funded, bootstrapped, self-published** project. Cool 3D World is listed as both developer and publisher ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3646020/Ultimate_Drummer/); [SteamDB](https://steamdb.info/app/3646020/)).

Supporting evidence from the Noclip interview ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8h3GW4Ze7pM)):
- Both describe themselves as **freelancers** (Baken in sound design and brand "white-label" VFX collaborations; Tessler in VFX). Cool 3D World "is definitely not a full-time job and a full-time provider… we do other things so that it can be like that" — i.e., the freelance/client work subsidizes the passion project.
- They chose to **learn the engine rather than hire/pay a programmer**, keeping costs near zero.
- No Kickstarter, publisher deal, or grant is mentioned anywhere in the sourcing; the $5.99 price and 1–2 hour length are consistent with a small self-financed passion release, not a funded studio product.

Distribution/marketing was DIY through their own channels plus one showcase slot: the demo was featured in **Computer Worlds**, an indie showcase run by developer **Gil Lawson** in collaboration with **Noclip 2** ([Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/indie-game-computer-world-showcase-steam-demo-2000678906)).

## 7. Similar & competing games

The key differentiator: most "music games" either **hit pre-authored notes** (Rock Band, Guitar Hero, Taiko) or **auto-sync action to a fixed track** (Hi-Fi Rush, Metal: Hellsinger). Very few let you **actively sequence/compose the music as a core system**. *Ultimate Drummer* sits in that rare lineage, fused with soulslike/DRPG structure.

**Drum/rhythm genre (dormant → mutated):** Guitar Hero (2005–2015) and Rock Band (2007–2015) crashed and were delisted (*Rock Band 4* delisted Oct 5, 2025); the formula lives on in **Fortnite Festival** (Harmonix/Epic) and **Taiko no Tatsujin** ([The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/news/789913/rock-band-4-is-being-delisted-on-its-10th-birthday-which-is-this-weekend); [Fortnite Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fortnite_Festival)). **Donkey Kong Jungle Beat** (2004) — drum-controller-driven platforming — is a direct conceptual ancestor of drum-powered traversal ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donkey_Kong_Jungle_Beat)).

**Music-*making*-as-mechanic (closest DNA):**
- **Fract OSC** (2014) — repair a broken world by rebuilding a giant synth/sequencer; the closest philosophical sibling ("restore a broken musical world"), but puzzle-only ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fract_OSC)).
- **Sound Shapes** (2012) — traversal builds a looping track in real time; the template for "movement composes music" ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sound_Shapes)).
- **Electroplankton** (2005) — pure generative music toy; clearest "the game *is* an instrument" precedent ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electroplankton)).
- **One Hand Clapping** (2021) — your real voice is the instrument ([review](https://epiloguegaming.com/one-hand-clapping-a-sonorous-sensation/)); **The Artful Escape** (2021) — psychedelic guitar-riff platformer, closest tonal cousin ([IGN](https://www.ign.com/articles/the-artful-escape-review)); **Wandersong** / **Chicory** — same "restore a broken/colorless world" indie pedigree ([Chicory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicory:_A_Colorful_Tale)).

**Rhythm-action hybrids (healthier, but reactive not generative):** Crypt of the NecroDancer, Hi-Fi Rush, BPM: Bullets Per Minute, Metal: Hellsinger, Soundfall, and **Everhood / Everhood 2** — the single closest **aesthetic + structural peer** (surreal, absurdist, "RPG," Guitar-Hero-style combat), though its music is authored per encounter, not sequenced live ([Everhood 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everhood_2)).

**Artist/animator-to-game-dev peers:** *Smiling Friends* creators (Newgrounds → Adult Swim → game tie-ins), **Talha & Jack Co.** (*Judero*/*Mashina*), and **David O'Reilly** (*Mountain*, *Everything*) — but *Ultimate Drummer* is unusually systems-heavy (character creator, combat, music sandbox) versus those more experimental/narrative projects.

## 8. Relevance to your Scale Navigator / MMODAW thinking

A few threads worth flagging given your own project:
- **Music-making as the verb, not note-matching** is exactly the rare niche you're aiming at. *Ultimate Drummer* validates appetite for it but deliberately buries the music-theory framing under "exploration platformer" — the same "remove the theory, make discovery the game" instinct in your ideation notes.
- **Their "hard to sell / people think it's a joke" problem** is a direct cautionary tale for a harmony-exploration game: an unclassifiable hybrid is a marketing liability even with a million-follower audience. Their fix — a **playable demo** — is what finally made it legible.
- **Their sequencer + "up to 6 musicians" sandbox** is a small-scale cousin of your Ensemble Jammer "constrain everyone to the shared state" idea, but single-player and beat-focused rather than harmony-driven — arguably leaving your harmonic-network angle unclaimed.
- **No artifact export** — unlike your "walk out with an Ableton session" north star, *Ultimate Drummer* keeps the music inside the game. That export-to-real-life loop remains a genuine differentiator you could own.
